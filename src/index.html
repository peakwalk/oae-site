Featured case study

Operator Asset Exchange Eliminates Critical Casing Delay

An offshore operator faced major difficulties running 9-5/8” casing through thinly laminated, unstable shales. The casing could not reach bottom, forcing a much shallower setting depth and leaving a long rathole that complicated the cement job as well as the next hole section.

With the next well in the batch sequence designed the same, the drilling team determined that a reamer shoe was essential to avoid repeating the problem. However, the next casing run was less than a week away, and no suitable reamer shoe was available locally.

Using Operator Asset Exchange, the Drilling Superintendent instantly identified a compatible reamer shoe held by an operator in a neighbouring country. Direct communication enabled rapid confirmation and a same-day commercial agreement.

The tool was dispatched the next morning and arrived in time, allowing the casing to be successfully run to bottom and preventing costly operational delays.
