1. What this policy covers

This Cookie Policy explains how Operator Asset Exchange (“we”, “us” or “our”) uses cookies and similar technologies on the public website.

2. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function, remember preferences, understand usage, and in some cases support analytics, security, and session management.

3. How we use cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • to enable essential website functionality and security;
  • to remember your cookie preferences;
  • to understand how visitors use the website and improve performance through Google Analytics; and
  • where applicable, to support platform-related functionality such as session continuity and authentication.

4. Cookie categories we use

Category Purpose Consent required?
Strictly necessary cookies Required for core website functions such as security, network management, session continuity, and remembering your cookie choices. No, where genuinely necessary for the website to function.
Functional cookies May be used to remember settings or preferences and improve the user experience. Depends on whether the cookie is strictly necessary or optional under applicable law.
Analytics cookies Help us understand website traffic, usage patterns, and page performance through Google Analytics so we can improve the site. Yes, these should only be used after you have accepted analytics cookies.

6. Consent and control

When you first visit the website, you should be presented with a cookie banner or preference tool that allows you to accept, reject, or manage non-essential cookies such as analytics cookies.

You can also control cookies through your browser settings, although blocking some cookies may affect how the website or certain functions operate.

7. Third-party cookies

Some cookies may be set by third-party services that support the website, including Google Analytics and other infrastructure or analytics providers. Those third parties control their own technologies and processing. You should review their own privacy and cookie information where relevant.
